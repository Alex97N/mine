A comment from Premending :

Essentially, this deck runs 6 Silent Magicians; the 3 actual copies, and the 3 Silent Burnings. It's almost never correct to play the Silent Burnings; the Silent Burnings are played to be discarded to your skill and activated from your GY. That's the whole point in playing them, and it should be very rare to play them in the natural way.

Sometimes it's correct to discard non-Silent Burning cards if you need fuel for Crystal Raigeki.

Sometimes it's correct to not use Unicorn's ability to fetch a copy of itself from the deck, particularly if you don't have any Crystal Raigekis. You need 1 or 2 copies in the deck so you can discard your Silent Burnings. Getting copies of itself from the GY is essentially free, and it's very rare for putting a second copy into play from your hand to be correct.

You should set your spellcasters before tributing them, particularly against decks with ways to get rid of them before you tribute them (primarily spellbooks). This gives them some protection in that most people use the auto yield, which doesn't give them a response window when you set them. It also protects them from ECon. However, if your opponent has 2 or 3 backrow and no frontrow, you could try baiting their Floodgate Trap Holes.

Reading backrow is very important to this deck. Drowning Mirror Force has been picking up a lot of popularity lately, so don't throw games that you'd otherwise win.
